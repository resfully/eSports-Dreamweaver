The Ideal Exercise Plan

Many people think that exercising will produce improved results instantly every time. However, the reality is that it takes time to build yourself up. That doesn't mean you have to work out every hour of the day though. In fact, you might even finish working out in an hour because of how hard it might be. So to exercise yourself properly, you have to start out slow and easy, then make things harder overtime.
You may be asking at this point, so what? What exercises am I exactly supposed to start with? Well, there are two types we will be covering. Cardio, - workouts that help build overall stamina - and Strengthening - workouts that build the muscles in the body.


Cardio workouts will generally be easy, as they can be something you do in your day to day life or as an easy exercise. These workouts will help improve the circulation of oxygen in your blood to all of your muscles, causing stamina to improve as time goes on while still building some muscle.


Jogging - 20 to 30 Minutes
As one of the best cardio workouts, this day-to-day workout is one of the most effective exercises (whether it be on a treadmill or outside) because it is something you do in your everyday life. To improve your stamina, it is best to do it at a speed that will increase the load on your heart, lungs, and different muscles.


10-15 Burpees, x2
Burpees is a very good exercise, as it works out the most muscles among the beginners level cardio workouts while still working the heart and lungs. To do this exercise, you first want to jump up and reach up with hands, then squat down on all fours, shoot your legs out into a push-up position, then finally go back to the squat position and jump up as said earlier.


30 Seconds of Jump Ropes, x2
For this workout to work, you will want to buy a jump rope. Otherwise, you can do this exercise in the air by acting like you are swinging a rope. This exercise is quite exceptional, as it is able to build your calves while still giving you a cardio workout. Also, it is a fun activity overall outside of an actual workout. If you are doing this without a jump rope, simply do small circles with your forearms to act like you are using one.


45 Seconds of Marching In Place
This exercise is an excellent alternative to jogging, as it is something you can do at home. To perform this, all you want to do is act like you are, as the name intends, marching but in the same spot while raising your knees high.



Strengthening workouts can generally be split into three categories - arm, core, and leg. Depending on what you may want to build more of, some exercises will do better at building a certain muscle than another. This type of workout somewhat relies on the Cardio workouts because you need the oxygen in your body to be circulated at a good speed in order to do these exercises for a longer amount of time.


Arm Workout - 30 Second Plank, x2 Plank is one of the most simple exercises, yet it works out a lot of muscles, the main ones being the transverses abdominis, rectus abdominis, internal oblique, and external oblique muscles. To perform this, you will simply want to get into a position similar to the push-up, except you will down on your forearms, and hold for the given amount of time.


Arm Workout - 15 Push-ups, x3
Being one of the most popular exercises, push-ups a number of muscles. However, they mainly work the triceps, pectoral muscles, and shoulders. To do this effectively, all you want to do is get into a high-plank position (as shown in the image), then go down until your arms reach a 90-degree angle and come back up once they do. Take a break between each set of 15 until completion.


Core Workout - 30 Seconds of Russian Twists
This exercise will be working your obliques and abdominal muscles, however it also tends to work the upper back, shoulders, and hip flexors muscles. To do Russian Twists, you will be sitting down will you legs out as if you are doing sit-ups, however your back will be off the ground. Like the name says, you will want to twist left and right while touching the ground with both hands.


Core Workout - 25 Leg Raises
Though it may sound like this will work the legs, this exercise will actually work the core muscles of your body. To perform this, all you want to do is lay down, then lift up your legs to a 90-degree angle, then bring them back down. Your hands can be in various places, such as under your butt, out to the side, or under your head. You also want to make sure that your legs are straight at all times.


Leg Workout - 30 Lunges
To do this workout, you will mainly have your hands on your hips. When doing this, you will simply put one foot forward and go down (as shown in the image). You will also want to alternate between your left and right leg so each leg gets some kind of workout. To avoid future injury, make sure your knee does not go past your toe line. Doing this exercise will work the hamstrings, quadriceps (thighs), and calves.


Leg Workout - 20 Squats, x2
For this simple workout, all you want to do is stand should-width apart and bend your knees to make it look like you are sitting down. Traditionally, you will want your hands in front of you or behind your head as you do each one. To avoid future injury, make sure your knees to not go past your toe line. This will work out your quadriceps, glutes, and hips.



Ideal Diet Plan

A diet plan is necessary to build the muscles needed to preform harder exercises. It is also needed to provide the right nutrients that are required to construct new muscle. This means that what you eat and how much you intake is essential to gain muscles. If you don't have a diet plan while doing strengthening trainings and such, it can actually lead to the severe loss of muscle tissue. This also suggests that having junk foods can severely inhibit the progress you could be making, as craving could easily takeover.

For the ideal plan however, you will want a good amount of everything. Whether it be fruits, vegetables, or meats, you do not want too much of one thing or the meal plan will not be balanced. You should try to have a relatively small snack in between each meal - such as breakfast and lunch - to replace some calories that may be lost throughout the day.
